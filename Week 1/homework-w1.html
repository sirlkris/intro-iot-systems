(4) Consider a house with an electric heater. How the electricity consumption of the heater would be affected by the air temperature? Is there a way to change this?

Answer: If the heater is temperature controlled, colder air temperature means that the heater must be on more often or longer times compared to warmer air temperature. This makes the heater to consume more electricity. If the air is warmer/close to the desired temperature, time that the heater must be on reduces. At the same time electricity consumption is reduced. By changing control type of the heater, the heater can be changed to output constant heat, so it does not follow air temperature thus consuming constant electrical energy.
